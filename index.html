How I work

Every piece starts with a real observation.

I find a genuine human moment, build a story around it, then figure out how the brand fits without breaking the narrative. The product shows up for 3 seconds, never 30. Tap any piece to see the creative approach.

685K

Riya Rants, Ep. 1

Emotional storytime
Expand
The insight: Everyone has that one saree in their mom's cupboard they've always wanted to wear. That memory is universal and deeply emotional. I built a confessional storytime around it where the brand mention becomes the punchline, not the pitch. WYLD cashback is woven into the story as a plot twist. Zero forced CTAs.
"The best brand integrations are the ones where removing the product would break the story, not improve it."

410K

POV: That First Date

Subtext-driven narrative
Expand
The insight: A first date where he can't pay is universally relatable. Instead of making the brand the hero, I made the girl the hero. She saves the moment casually with a WYLD card. The product becomes a character trait, not an ad.
"Show the product solving a real human moment, not a marketing problem. If the audience feels like they're watching an ad, you've already lost."

"Thoda Piche Ja"

Romantic meet-cute
Expand
The insight: Two people backing into each other while on video calls is simple, cinematic, and immediately shareable. A rom-com beat compressed into 60 seconds. WYLD shows up at the billing counter for under 3 seconds. No voiceover, no explanation. The story carries itself.
"A good integration is invisible on first watch and obvious on second. If someone shares it because of the story, the brand travels for free."
